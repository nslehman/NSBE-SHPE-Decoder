NSBE-SHPE Member Spotlight
Mohammed Alam, or Abir as the eBoard knows him, is the current Academic Excellence chair for the NSBE and SHPE chapters at Lehman College. Abir, who is a Computer Science student in his final semester at Lehman, was recently extended a full-time offer by Capital One!

Abir also interned for Northrop Gruman over the summer as a software development intern and will be looking to apply the technical skills he acquired at his internship out in sunny California with Northrop Gruman at Capital One.

Abir's hardworking atitude, humility, and kindness are some of the many attributes that made him the professional that he is today. These characteristics are also the reason Abir is our final NSBE-SHPE spotlight member of the week! Thank you for all that you have contributed to NSBE-SHPE, Abir. We wish you all the best!

Grown With Google
To apply for a scholarship, you need to be at least 18 years old and live in the US. You'll begin by choosing your learning path, either Web Developer or Android Developer. From there, once you're accepted, we'll place you in one of two tracks, depending on your existing skills and experience, either Beginner or Intermediate. After that, the learning begins! Finally, top students from each track will earn full scholarships to one of our Android or Web Development Nanodegree programs.

Women Techmakers Scholars Program
Women Techmakers Scholars will each receive $10,000 USD (for students in the US) or $5,000 CAD (for students in Canada) for the 2018-2019 academic year. A group of female undergraduate and graduate students will be chosen from the applicant pool, and scholarships will be awarded based on the strength of each candidate's academic background and demonstrated leadership and impact on the community.
