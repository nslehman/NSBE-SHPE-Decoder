2018 Technical HBCU@SXSW Student Application!       

Last year Opportunity Hub sponsored 50 minority college students to go to SXSW.

This year their goal is to sponsor 500 of the top minority engineering, computer science, innovative and entrepreneurially-minded talent from HBCU's (Historically Black Colleges and Universities) and PWI's (Predominantly White Institutions) across America.

Accepted students receive a SXSW Interactive badge and all expenses paid trip to Austin, TX. Travel and housing are provided.

The cost to sponsor a student is $3,000.00 US.

We encourage you to apply for an opportunity to be considered as a part of our 2018 cohort.

For consideration for acceptance in the 2018 cohort, students must be an African American or Latinx American college student, preferably, but not exclusive, enrolled in a Historically Black College and University (HBCU) or Minority Serving Institutions (MSI). We also encourage minorities at Predominantly White Institutions (PWI's) to apply as well.

Students must complete the application below in its entirety.

Students will also be required to submit a resume and references from the engineering, entrepreneurial or innovator lead at their college or university; and influencers in their community. Per our growing list of corporate partners, selection will be determined by declared degree program, coding skills, sales skills, business development skills, marketing skills, project management and agile skills and ultimately your ability to communicate how you will add value to the innovation economy after SXSW and beyond.

Please take your time in preparing the best application and resume to submit.

NSBE-SHPE Member Spotlight
Daniel Adeyanju joined the NSBE chapter at Lehman College back in the Fall semester of 2014, and even after graduating from Lehman in 2016 with a second Bachelors degree (this time in Computer Science), has remained an active and vital member of the chapter.

Currently, Daniel is out in sunny California working as an iOS Developer for Macy’s, where his day to day involves working on features specific to the mobile app. Daniel also interned for Macy’s as a Software Engineer in the summer of 2016 before becoming a full-time employee.

“I joined NSBE because I knew that it would provide me with the resources and support system to succeed and because of its mission to increase the number of culturally responsible Black engineers. I truly believe in lifting while you climb and NSBE was and is an opportunity to do that, ” said Daniel when asked what made him join NSBE.

Those who have spoken to Daniel can agree that some of his greatest traits as a person are his initiative and his ability to think of the bigger picture. "When Dana, our chapter founder, sent out flyers with her direct phone number I reached out. The rest was history. Since then I attended two national conventions and two regional conventions. The money and time I invested in those events were nothing compared to the feeling of seeing 10s of thousands of black engineers. I participated in NSBEs national hackathon as well and made strong connections with many individuals that last today. These experiences challenged me to look beyond my classes and see what I really had to do to get a job as a software engineer. NSBE leveled the playing field for me." 

"Ultimately NSBE gave me my first foot in the door at Macy's as an intern which led to my first software engineering job several months later in San Francisco! I have a lot of love for NSBE - now NS - and will continue to support Black and Brown engineers from my second alma mater, Lehman College." We at NS have a lot of love for you too, Daniel!

Whether sharing advice or constantly posting opportunities in our Slack chat, Daniel's dedication and support to NSBE-SHPE's mission during his college years and after are just a few of many reasons he is our Spotlight Member of the week! We at NSBE-SHPE would like to THANK YOU for all that you do for the chapter!
